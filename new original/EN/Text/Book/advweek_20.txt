Adventurer's Weekly #20: Recycling, Yowyn Correspondent Celia,10,1

<i>Welcome to Adventurer's Weekly, where we cover topics near and dear to your brave hearts.</i>


This week, I'm writing to spread the word about recycling!

Living in rural Yowyn is great for my health, and a big part of that is the pristine environment; the gods have given us this land, and it's our job to steward it! Maintaining our natural country living means cleaning up after ourselves!
{p}
There's also hanging the occasional city-slicker we catch littering in the forest — sending a strong message is important!

Trash is unavoidable, we all produce junk. But by recycling, we reduce how much we throw away. And it's better than an anti-littering campaign, because it doesn't make everyone grumpy and we get lots of materials!

That's today's topic!


<b>Sorting</b>

Every morning, I sort my trash into three containers: the combustible waste box, non-combustible waste box, and compost box. They don't need to be shared to work! Everyone should have at least one set!
{p}
Just in case the boxes are full, I've also got a garbage dump sign to catch spillover so it doesn't scatter all over.

Not everyone is as civic-minded as me — littering can still happen at low civility. But a good waste sorting campaign, some public morals, and of course, arranging for the litterer to spend some time on the gallows works wonders!

One day, <b>everyone's</b> trash will be sorted correctly into the right containers, and then we will <b>all</b> be clean! Let's work together to be gentle on Ylva!


<b>Recycling</b>

Now that we've collected lots of trash, it's time to introduce Mr. Hammer!
{p}
Mr. Hammer disassembles things into useful materials, so introduce him to your rubbish and be surprised! You can break down anything you don't need into wood, ingots, textures — even obtain rare materials!

Today, I hammered all my non-burnable trash and wove the chunks into textures and string, then took apart some kettles to get ingots for making bolts! Someone had thrown away some leaf dishes, so I hammered those back into large leaves and made rope!

Before long, I had several large backpacks, very popular during harvest time! The only material my garbage didn't provide are the skins, which I got from the butcher! If any of the adventurers who bought my backpacks knew they were made from trash, they might've complained… but when you think about it, why? There's nothing wrong with breathing new life into old goods!
{p}
There're also other ways to use junk, like turning it into fertilizer, sacrificing it to Lady Jure, or fulfilling requests! Be creative, and you'll never have to throw anything out! Recycling old appliances to avoid disposal fees? That's me!
{p}
By embracing recycling, we not only pollute less, but also save money! But what do you do with junk you can't recycle? Well, you can dispose of it in a trash can, and earn ecopo for not littering or dumping it in the shipping bin! Here in Yowyn, you can buy lots of great things at the ecopo vendor!

Now, I've got to say goodbye, as I'm going fishing! Bait? Made with bones recycled from my trash, of course!
