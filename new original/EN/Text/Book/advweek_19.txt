Terrors that flap in the night!

Predators lurking in the dark!

Tales of vampires are date back centuries. With these ancient undead recently sighted all over Tyris, it's time to separate fact from fiction!


<b>Blood</b>

Vampiric thirst is legendary; all the tales agree that they cannot eat normal food and instead must drink blood. Almost anyone can be a victim, so long as it can bleed; constructs are obvious exceptions, but in some tales bleed-immune creatures like hedgehogs escape predation too.
{p}
Vampires view blood as we do wine, morbidly declaring individuals to have certain flavors; they can sense someone's blood traits at a glance, with experienced vampires discerning more information.

Vampires benefit from stronger victims akin to a quality meal; this is further increased by the vampire's learning and blood suck ability. Victims' reactions to predation vary from shame and disgust, to finding the act pleasurable; this has led to stories of romances between vampires and humans, where the latter is kept around as a delicious and nutritious meal!

I don't understand it myself, but people like these kinds of novels, so…
{p}
Curiously, seasoning food with ketchup makes it fit for vampires. The Mages' Guild claims this implies that vampires don't need blood per se, but rather some metaphysical aspect since food with the <i>appearance</i> of blood is enough. It's not as filling as actual blood, but is more inconspicuous than seizing someone's neck or carrying blood bags around.

{p}
<b>Other Abilities</b>

* Being lords of the night, vampires gain stats from 5 pm to 5 am; this change is swift but not instant. Whether a vampire is actually exposed to sunlight doesn't matter; like ketchup food, their sensitivity to day and night even underground suggests the connection is metaphysical rather than material.

* Vampires often transform into bats to travel; in this form, they do not fear sunlight and are highly evasive, but are also extremely weak and frail. Despite being fast and stealthy, a single lucky hit may easily kill them.

{p}
<b>Identification and Protection</b>

Many higher undead are indistinguishable from the living at a glance, so it falls to the discerning eye to unmask vampires:

*Vampires will stay wet to avoid burning in sunlight. Beware of people pouring liquids over themselves, walking in rain, or swimming for no reason!

*Vampires are weak to fire and often equip far too much fire resistance equipment; someone clad from head to toe in dragonscale is definitely suspicious!

*There are headgear enchantments that protect undead from sunlight — a dead giveaway.
{p}
*Beware of bats carrying tools, weapons, or other items! Being transformed does not prevent vampires from acting normally!

*Some vampires fearful of bad meals have turned to science — randomly jabbing strangers with blood draw syringes is not normal behaviour.

*Vampires negligent enough to doze off in sunlight unprotected suffer greatly. Anyone insisting on so-called "novelty" coffin beds warrants concern.
{p}
Most myths about repelling vampires have little basis; for example, the long-held belief that Jure water melts vampires is false. However, sanctified figurines of Jure, available at Noyel's Holy Night Festival, do discourage vampire predation. Common measures against undead — fire, holy and undead bane — are also effective.

Vampires are not innately evil, with records of several model citizens; battling the temptation to see everyone as merely a meal is a noble effort of will. Unfortunately, most vampires don't make that distinction; following several recent deaths, the Palmian city guard urges all to report any suspicious activity.
