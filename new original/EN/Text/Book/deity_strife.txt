Lesser Deities: Strife,Theolucia - Scholar of History,0

Little is known about the god of strife. Though Yevan is an ancient deity said to have taken part in the wars against the Forgotten Gods, there are remarkably few surviving texts that record his name. Yet as war spread in the wake of the Zanan–Eulderna conflict and the founding of Yerl, Yevan’s worship has, over the past few centuries, gradually gathered support among radical revolutionaries and militant sects.

In stark contrast to Opatos, who is widely revered among warriors and soldiers, those who venerate Yevan are often treated as heretics or extremists. His creed has never become a mainstream faith, but it is marked by an intensity uncommon even among the devout.
{p}
<b>Rogue God of Conflict</b>

Unlike most lesser deities, Yevan has no devoted race, no unified order, and no organized priesthood. Nor does the god himself seem to place much importance on spreading his influence. If anything, he appears far more fascinated by the affairs of mortals.

Yevan imparts only a single teaching to those who seek his guidance:

<i>Overcome your challenges. The path you take is your own.</i>

Such ambiguity is rare within the pantheon. From this lone maxim, however, the core of Yevan’s philosophy can be inferred. He urges mortals to decide their own way of being, to press forward by conviction, and to refine themselves through struggle.
{p}
<b>Strange Rumors</b>

To deepen my understanding, I have gathered several accounts and rumors that may relate to the god of strife:

- Where Winds Rest

Whispers speak of a secluded site on the continent of Tyris where one of the Forgotten Gods now stripped of power lies sealed away. The place is said to be watched over by a mysterious figure tied to Yevan. Some insist this watcher is constantly assessing passersby, searching for a champion strong enough to defeat what sleeps behind the seal.
{p}
- Shrines of Trial

Deep within certain Nefias stand ominous shrines that summon vicious monsters upon touch. Their design bears an uncanny resemblance to relics of Yevan worship dating back to the Sixth Era. If one accepts the belief that battle against the strong is itself a trial meant to be overcome, these shrines may represent a fragment of the god’s power.

- The Dreaming Gladiator

A renowned warrior of Karune once boasted that “no being alive could match his strength.” Soon after, he began to dream of a hooded, scarred man clad in archaic garments—an adversary who challenged him again and again, and each time defeated him. The writings that survive claim the warrior’s arrogance was broken by these dreams, and that he thereafter reached greater heights than before.
{p}
- The Vanished Soldiers

During the great war between Zanan and Eulderna, many Zanan followers of Yevan fought on the front lines. Among those who fought most fiercely and fell grievously wounded, some were said to vanish without a trace by the following day. Soldiers on both sides whispered that the lord of strife had taken those he favored into his realm.

No conclusive evidence binds these tales together. Yet if we assume even a portion of them are true, a consistent pattern emerges: Yevan seeks those who continue to challenge, advance, and progress.

To many, the god of strife is an heretical bringer of death. However, to his devotees, he may also be something else entirely: a brutal yet empathetic supporter of the mortals, holding the utmost faith in their limitless potential.
