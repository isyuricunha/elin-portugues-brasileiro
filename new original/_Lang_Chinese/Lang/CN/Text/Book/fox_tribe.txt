The Fox Tribes,Theolucia - Scholar of History,0

Owing to recent interest in the mifu and nefu, especially the latter, I've chosen this topic for my thesis in the hope it'll be read by more than my fellow academics. If this will help those visiting the fox tribes avoid difficulties, I'll be satisfied.


<b>Origins</b>

Little is certain about the fox tribes' origins — their own folklore states they've lived in North Tyris for at least all of Sierre Terre. A popular theory amongst adventurers is that they descend from children born of the god Kizuami's relations with humans during the fifth and and sixth eras of Ylva.
{p}
However, if this were true, the fox tribes would have survived multiple mass extinctions. Be it the end of the sixth or seventh ages, the quiet end of the ninth age, the fall of Rem-Ido and the miracle of Vindale in the tenth — for a people to repeatedly evade such cataclysmic events unnoticed is extremely implausible. Furthermore, their own legends state that Kizuami took these demigod children away from Ylva as his apostles, which contradicts this theory.

Perhaps the lack of information is a clue in itself: I believe that whatever the fox tribes' origins, it must have happened in a particularly chaotic period of Ylva for them to have appeared so suddenly without record.
{p}
The fox tribes themselves speak of arriving in North Tyris from a distant continent unknown to us. This is plausible as much of eastern and southern Ylva remains unexplored, but with Vindale impassable to humans and Yerl feuding with Eulderna, we may never know.


<b>Human Relations</b>

The first official record of the fox tribes was during King Zashim's reign: after travellers to Larna spotted a new village at the mountain's base, the crown dispatched troops and later tax assessors to investigate. While the mifu did not have money, they possessed rice in abundance; as one of the officials wrote: "they were so willing to foist their grain upon us, we suspected an ulterior motive."
{p}
The resulting agreement stands to this day: Mifu Village is taxed in rice, measured in a person's yearly ration of grain according to their customs. This goes toward sustaining the crown's armies and stockpiles for disaster relief; in return, Palmia has granted the village autonomy.

While it's concerning that the mifu do not recognize Palmia's authority and answer only to their god, monarchs since Zashim have wisely chosen not to pursue the matter of their loyalties nor set themselves against the divine, and instead continue to enjoy yearly shipments of grain.

Nefu Village is another matter. Because it is so remote, neither Palmia nor Mayroon have attempted to claim it; the trip is not worth any taxes that might be levied from what is primarily a religious site. This independence suits the nefu just fine; they too only answer to their goddess, and have the strength of arms to keep things that way.
{p}
<b>Dos and Don'ts</b>

Please keep these guidelines in mind if you visit, especially during one of their yearly festivals:

Do:

* Be polite.
* Keep to the areas open to tourists, whom they consider pilgrims.
* Remove your shoes when going indoors.
* Appreciate the villages' natural beauty and unique culture.
* Leave only footprints and bring away only memories.
{p}
Don't:

* Disrespect the sacred cherry blossom tree.
* Openly stare at the foxes.
* Touch their ears and tails — they are so sensitive as to be erogenous.
* Swim in the river or waterfall.
* Ask where the milk on sale comes from.
* Raise your voice unnecessarily; the fox tribes are soft-spoken peoples.
* Disrespect the sacred cherry blossom tree. Please, for your own sake, don't take this lightly.
